Introduction

Fractal geometry is a branch of mathematics that explores complex patterns that are self-similar across different scales. Unlike traditional Euclidean geometry, which studies simple shapes like lines, circles, and polygons, fractal geometry focuses on irregular, fragmented, and infinitely detailed shapes found both in mathematical constructs and in nature.
